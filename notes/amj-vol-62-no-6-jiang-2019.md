# Can Joy Buy You Money? The Impact of the Strength, Duration, and Phases of an Entrepreneur’s Peak Displayed Joy on Funding Performance

**Abstract**
Does displaying positive emotions (e.g., joy) during a funding pitch help an entrepreneur gain more financial support? Past research has approached this question mostly by treating emotional displays as static and focusing on the overall or average levels of displayed emotions. However, emotional displays are temporally dynamic and more salient in some moments or phases than others. Drawing from gestalt characteristics and event system theories, we take a dynamic approach to examine the “peak” moments of entrepreneurs’ displayed joy—specifically, the strength and duration of peak displayed joy during different phases of a pitch. We analyzed data from over eight million frames in 1,460 pitch videos, using the latest facial expression analysis technology. The findings unveil the benefit of pitching with a greater level of peak displayed joy, especially during the beginning and ending phases of a pitch. Moreover, the amount of time an entrepreneur spends at the peak level of his or her displayed joy has an inverted U-shaped relationship with funding performance. This research highlights not only the importance of investigating emotion temporal dynamics in the interpersonal context, but also the unique research opportunities provided by facial expression analyses in understanding complex management phenomena.

**Research Question**
The paper asks whether displaying positive emotion, specifically joy, during an entrepreneur’s funding pitch helps gain financial support. It focuses on whether the strength, duration, and beginning/ending phases of peak displayed joy are associated with crowdfunding performance.

**Mechanism Process**
- IV(s): Peak displayed joy level; total length of time at peak displayed joy; beginning-, middle-, and ending-phase peak displayed joy and peak duration.
- DV(s): Logged funding amount and logged number of backers.
- Mediators: Not directly tested; theorized mechanisms include emotional contagion, attribution of positive affect to the entrepreneur, and inferences about the entrepreneur’s potential for success.
- Moderators: Temporal phase of the pitch, especially beginning and ending phases, is theorized through primacy and recency effects.

The mechanism is that a stronger peak display of joy becomes especially salient and memorable to pitch viewers, making viewers more likely to experience positive affect and infer favorable qualities about the entrepreneur. Event system theory adds that duration and phase matter: brief peak joy can intensify attention and support, but prolonged peak joy can backfire by appearing excessive, unprofessional, or overly optimistic. The empirical results support positive associations for peak displayed joy, especially in the beginning and ending phases, and an inverted U-shaped association for peak duration in the full pitch and beginning phase.

**Theoretical Contribution**
The paper connects emotion temporal dynamics research with the interpersonal influence of displayed emotions. It contributes by showing that displayed emotion should not be treated only as a static average level; peak strength, duration, and phase can matter for observers’ decisions in an entrepreneurial funding context.

It also contributes methodologically by using automated facial expression analysis to measure dynamic displayed emotion frame by frame in pitch videos. This provides a scalable visual method for studying fine-grained emotional displays in management research.

**Practical Implication**
The paper suggests that entrepreneurs using crowdfunding pitch videos should attend not only to whether they display joy, but also to when and how long they display peak joy. Higher peak displayed joy is associated with better funding outcomes, but extended time at peak joy can harm funding performance.

Entrepreneurs should pay particular attention to the beginning and ending phases of pitches, where peak displayed joy is especially consequential. The authors caution that entrepreneurs should avoid prolonged peak joy, including in the beginning phase.

**Limitations**
The study focuses on peak states of displayed joy while controlling for other temporal characteristics such as ending states, trend, and average. The authors note that other temporal properties, including emotion covariation, inertia, and the ordering of positive and negative emotions, remain open for future work.

The research examines joy and controls for average negative emotions, but it does not analyze the temporal characteristics of negative emotions or subtler positive emotions such as gratitude, passion, and contentment. The crowdfunding setting also did not allow the authors to collect granular individual-backer data on motivations or support decisions.

**Future Research**
Future research could examine other temporal dynamics of displayed emotions, including covariation, inertia, and the ordering of positive and negative emotional moments. Studies could also analyze negative emotions or subtler positive emotions in contexts where those emotions are more prominent.

The authors also encourage future work in traditional pitching contexts where individual investors can be observed or interviewed. Such designs could test the affective and cognitive mechanisms linking entrepreneurs’ peak displayed joy to each viewer’s funding decision.
