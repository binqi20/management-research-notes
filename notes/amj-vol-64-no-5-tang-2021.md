# Balancing the Yin and Yang: TMT Gender Diversity, Psychological Safety, and Firm Ambidextrous Strategic Orientation in Chinese High-Tech SMEs

**Abstract**
This paper offers a novel theoretical account of why and when top management team (TMT) gender diversity lends strategic advantage. Building on social role theory, we develop a moderated-mediation model showing: (a) TMT psychological safety mediates the effect of TMT gender diversity on firm ambidextrous strategic orientation (ASO) (why), and (b) firm slack moderates this mediated effect (when). We tested our model in the context of Chinese high-tech smalland medium-sized enterprises. After confirming gender differences in social role–based proclivities at the TMT level, a multiwave survey study of 373 members from 120 TMTs showed that TMT gender diversity positively affects ASO via TMT psychological safety, and this mediated effect is stronger when firm slack is lower than higher.We further interviewed 23 top managers to supplement key quantitative results. Our study advances upper echelons researchon TMTgenderdiversity in two ways. First, ithighlights the gender-specific interpersonal benefit of TMT gender diversity, which is markedly distinct from the cognitive-variety argument associated generically with TMT demographic diversity. Second, it considers both men and women in TMTs in a more balanced manner, thereby offering an alternative account to the female-focused theorization of the positive strategic implications of TMT gender diversity.

**Research Question**
The paper asks why and when TMT gender diversity generates strategic advantage for firms. Specifically, it examines whether TMT psychological safety explains the effect of TMT gender diversity on firm ambidextrous strategic orientation and whether firm slack changes the strength of this mediated relationship.

**Mechanism Process**
- IV(s): TMT gender diversity, measured using Blau's index.
- DV(s): Firm ambidextrous strategic orientation, based on exploration and exploitation ratings.
- Mediators: TMT psychological safety.
- Moderators: Firm slack resources, which moderate both the TMT gender diversity-to-psychological safety path and the psychological safety-to-ASO path.

The mechanism is that gender-diverse TMTs combine women's communal, socially sensitive proclivities with men's agentic, voice-raising proclivities. This complementarity fosters psychological safety, understood as both mutual trust and support and the ability to speak up and challenge others. TMT psychological safety then helps members differentiate and integrate exploration and exploitation, supporting ASO. The mediated effect is stronger when firm slack is lower, because resource scarcity makes constructive TMT dynamics more important.

**Theoretical Contribution**
The paper challenges the generic cognitive-variety explanation for TMT demographic diversity by arguing that the strategic value of TMT gender diversity is interpersonal and gender-specific. It contributes to upper echelons research by showing that TMT psychological safety transmits the relationship between gender diversity and ASO. It also shifts theorizing away from a female-centered "more women is better" account toward a gender-balance account that treats men and women as complementary participants in TMT dynamics.

**Practical Implication**
The findings suggest that firms should treat TMT gender balance as relevant to the interpersonal quality of strategic decision-making, not only as a representation or cognitive-diversity issue. Managers seeking ambidextrous strategy should pay attention to whether TMT members can both support one another and openly challenge ideas. The results also imply that gender-diverse TMTs may be especially valuable when firms face resource scarcity.

**Limitations**
The authors state that the SME setting may limit generalizability to large and established firms. They also note that the Chinese context may limit generalizability to other countries, including countries with different levels of gender egalitarianism. The study focuses on ASO and firm slack, leaving other strategic outcomes and other external environmental moderators for future work.

**Future Research**
Future research could test the interpersonal benefits of TMT gender balance in large firms and in countries with different cultural gender norms. Studies could examine other outcomes shaped by TMT psychological safety, such as entrepreneurial orientation or other strategic orientations. Additional work could also test whether munificence, dynamism, uncertainty, or other external conditions moderate the mediated effect of TMT gender diversity.
