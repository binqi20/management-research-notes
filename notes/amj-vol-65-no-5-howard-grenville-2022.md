# From the Editors—That’s Important, Interesting, and Generative: Winners of the AMJ 2021 Best Paper Award and 2022 Research Impact Award

**Abstract**
Not reported in paper

**Research Question**
Not reported in paper

**Mechanism Process**

- Core argument: Important and interesting research need not be trade-offs; problem- or phenomenon-driven curiosity can motivate and sustain research that is both societally relevant and theoretically rigorous.
- Motivation: Each award-winning paper started a new conversation by asking fundamentally new questions about an understudied phenomenon (religiosity and the gender wage gap; basic science on diseases of the poor) rather than joining a mature theoretical conversation.
- Process: A surprising main effect pushed the authors toward greater methodological rigor, transparency, and theory building, often using extensive international data.
- Counterintuitive commonality: Both studies illuminated universal principles and global commonalities (rather than differences), making global sampling powerful for theoretical and empirical contribution.
- Generativity: Publication is the beginning, not the end, of a generative, cocreated line of inquiry sustained by authors, audiences, editors, and reviewers.

The editorial distills, in the authors' own words, how curiosity about important yet understudied phenomena, combined with global data and methodological transparency, can produce research that is generative of further impact and study.

**Theoretical Contribution**
As a From-the-Editors essay, the piece does not advance a formal theory but articulates a programmatic stance for AMJ: that "phenomenon-driven, theoretically novel, rigorous, and societally important" research can connect management to grand challenges and societal conversations without sacrificing theoretical and empirical rigor.

**Practical Implication**
The editors encourage management scholars to be confident and courageous in pursuing problem-driven questions of societal importance, to leverage multidisciplinary methods and global data (noting about 48% of recent AMJ studies use non-U.S. samples for theory testing), and to trust that editors and reviewers will support bold, generative work.

**Limitations**
Not reported in paper

**Future Research**
The editors invite authors to look for and generate data from contexts that might reveal surprising commonalities and differences, and to pursue generative research that prompts and propels further novel and important inquiry on societally relevant topics.
