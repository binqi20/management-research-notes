**Mechanism Process**
- Core argument: Theory, methods, and data are inexorably intertwined; the foundation of a strong research discipline hinges on the strength of theory–method–data alignment, and innovations in any one element often drive advances in the others.
- Familiar-but-underused approaches: report and interpret observed power; adopt Bayesian techniques as alternatives to NHST; leverage longitudinal (panel) data and exogenous events with designs such as difference-in-difference and interrupted time series for stronger causal inference; revisit constructs and measurement (primary instruments, machine learning, wearable sensors).
- Less familiar but established approaches: incorporate computational modeling to formalize verbal theories and derive predictions for dynamic/complex phenomena; use latent mixture models to theorize about and detect unobserved heterogeneity (subpopulations, intersectionality, boundary conditions).
- Cross-paradigm integration: embrace outliers as theoretically informative rather than trimming them, using qualitative comparative analysis or multiple case study to interpret them; consider "off-diagonal" research — quantitative data with inductive methods (e.g., quantitative grounded theory) and qualitative data with deductive design (e.g., alternate templates, pattern matching).
- Boundary conditions: data and results are often more variable than acknowledged; robust theories require systematically making sense of heterogeneity (in outliers, subpopulations, and observed power).

The editorial advances a chain-of-strength metaphor: any weak link — design, method, or interpretation — threatens the whole research chain, and "brilliant theory that cannot be tested with methods and data might be considered a form of science fiction." The authors organize their suggestions into three buckets (familiar-but-underused tools, methods well established outside AMJ, and qualitative–quantitative integration) and argue that each can sharpen causal inference and theory development by acknowledging variability and heterogeneity rather than smoothing them away.

**Theoretical Contribution**
The editorial provides a programmatic agenda for AMJ authors and reviewers: it explicitly names underutilized approaches (observed power, Bayesian techniques, DD with exogenous events, computational modeling, latent mixture analysis, outlier-as-evidence, off-diagonal designs, modernized measurement) and frames them as concrete levers for strengthening theory–method–data alignment. By complementing Grégoire et al. (2024) on novel data sources, it consolidates a vocabulary for cross-fertilization across micro/macro/qualitative subfields and across cognate disciplines (economics, sociology, psychology, medicine).

**Practical Implication**
Researchers are encouraged to (a) routinely report and discuss observed power and consider Bayesian credibility intervals, (b) exploit longitudinal data and exogenous shocks (e.g., COVID-19, the great recession) for difference-in-difference and interrupted time-series designs, (c) translate verbal theories into computational models before empirical testing, (d) use latent mixture methods to model heterogeneity, (e) treat outliers as theoretical opportunities rather than nuisances, (f) explore quantitative grounded theory and alternate-templates pattern matching, and (g) update construct measurement using primary instruments, machine learning, and digital/wearable sensors.

**Limitations**
The authors explicitly acknowledge that the discussion is "not meant to be comprehensive" and that no individual or group of authors can claim expertise in every method discussed; their collective expertise does not cover the entirety of the methods landscape, and the selection of approaches reflects the editorial team's judgment about what is currently underutilized at AMJ.

**Future Research**
Future management research can enhance the rigor and robustness of causal inference by deploying these diverse approaches in combination. The authors call for ongoing dialogue and mutual learning across methodological traditions and disciplines to facilitate adoption of these underutilized approaches.
