# Special Research Forum—Our Transformational Era: Implications for Management and Organizations

**Abstract**
Not reported in paper

**Research Question**
How are technological advances and mounting societal challenges transforming management and organizations, and how can scholars study these ongoing shifts—including by "researching forward"—to challenge, change, and advance existing theory or develop new theory?

**Hypotheses / Propositions**
Not reported in paper

**Mechanism Process**
- Technology and the changing nature of knowledge and work: how algorithmic systems, digital tools, and categorization processes are reshaping expertise, innovation, and learning.
- Identity, inclusion, and human flourishing in the workplace: how evolving organizational practices and ideologies influence worker dignity, mental health, and equitable career advancement.
- Governance, stakeholders, and the reconfiguration of institutional relationships: how organizations respond to legitimacy threats, community mobilization, and shifting stakeholder expectations.

The editorial organizes 10 Special Research Forum studies into three thematic clusters and argues that they share a deeper commonality: the urgent need to rethink foundational assumptions about how organizations operate, for whom they exist, and how they should evolve amid dramatic external and internal shifts. It frames organizational transformation as simultaneously technological, epistemological, and deeply human, and introduces "researching forward" as a stance that narrows the gap between retrospective theorizing and the forward-looking concerns of practice.

**Data & Measures**
Not reported in paper

**Key Findings**
Not reported in paper

**Theoretical Contribution**
The forum demonstrates the analytical potential of bridging micro- and macro-level insights and integrating diverse theoretical lenses—including identity theory, cognitive science, institutional theory, collective action, and knowledge-based views—to understand organizations in transformation. Beyond diagnosis, the editors argue the Special Research Forum offers direction: new models, practices, and priorities for scholars and practitioners. The editorial advances the notion of "researching forward" (real-time, prospective, and engaged designs) as a way to make scholarship more relevant to unfolding transformational trends.

**Practical Implication**
The editors urge scholars to move beyond studying phenomena retrospectively and to articulate the practical and policy implications of their work more clearly to a broad and diverse audience, engaging more directly with practice. For practitioners who seek to lead, study, and engage with organizations, the forum offers new models, practices, and priorities to help navigate technological, human, and institutional transformation in ways that are adaptive, ethical, and future-facing.

**Limitations**
Not reported in paper

**Future Research**
The editors highlight significant opportunities for scholars to study the nonlinearity of institutional and sociopolitical change and its consequences for organizations and for humanity's ability to address collective grand challenges. They encourage "researching forward" through real-time studies, collaborative inquiry, field experiments, prospective case studies, and "racing designs," and call for further work on the links among work arrangements, mental illness, and individual and organizational strategies for managing it.
