**Theoretical Contribution**
Writing as outgoing editor-in-chief at the close of the 23rd editorial term, Gruber frames the present as a "transformational era" of unprecedented technological disruption and machine-learning advance that reshapes not only the phenomena management scholars study but also how research is conducted, moving toward an "instant research paradigm" in which data, analyses, new insights, and reports become available almost instantaneously. The editorial also articulates an evolving conception of the scientific-journal editor's role: digital technologies extend a journal's basic mission beyond one-way communication of scientific advances toward a livelier discourse with scholarly audiences and novel ways to communicate scientific knowledge.

**Practical Implication**
Addressing the AMJ scholarly community, the editorial encourages authors to conduct and submit studies that employ new empirical methods and novel sources of data, and points to concrete supports the 23rd team established — a board of consulting methodological experts, an expanded manuscript portfolio that now includes methodological articles designed to advance theory, and the AMJ Management Research Canvas as a practical tool for the research design and manuscript preparation phases. Reflecting on the machine-learning analysis of rejected submissions, it closes with the message that openness to the feedback of peers and perseverance matter a lot in academic life.
