**Theoretical Contribution**
As an editorial reflecting on the 23rd AMJ editorial term, the piece does not make a theoretical contribution in the conventional sense. It documents how the editorial team pursued the theme "Our transformational times and their implications for management and organizations," built out a methods-oriented manuscript portfolio (including new research methods articles designed to advance theory), and launched digital initiatives to extend AMJ's reach and discourse with scholarly audiences. It positions editorial stewardship itself as a strategic activity for shaping what counts as rigorous, relevant management scholarship in an era of rapid technological and methodological change.

**Practical Implication**
The editorial signals to prospective authors that AMJ welcomes studies employing machine-learning methods, novel data sources, and work on sustainability, technology/AI, and inclusion, and that fast, developmental turnaround (close to a 58-day average for fully reviewed first submissions) remains a core service commitment. It also points authors to practical tools developed during the term, including the AMJ Management Research Canvas and a series of editorials on research design, multimethod research, interdisciplinary collaboration, and qualitative and quantitative empirics. Finally, it encourages perseverance and openness to peer feedback, noting that many rejected AMJ submissions ultimately find strong outlets elsewhere.

**Future Research**
The editorial invites the incoming 24th editorial team, led by Quinetta Roberson, to continue expanding AMJ's global footprint and digital presence, to further integrate new methods (including machine learning) into management scholarship, and to advance work on sustainability, AI and technology, and inclusion as central concerns of the transformational era. It also suggests further attention to regions with historically lower AMJ activity identified through the interactive submission map.
