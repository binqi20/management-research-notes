# Innovating for impact: Harnessing business model innovation to tackle grand challenges

**Abstract**
Highlighting BMI's role as a boundary-spanning, collaborative approach to innovation, we demonstrate its potential to drive sustainable solutions by rethinking value creation, delivery, and capture mechanisms. We examine how BMI may foster resilience and help firms and organizations adapt to global volatility, integrate ethical AI, and advance sustainability in business ecosystems. We offer a roadmap for future inquiry into BMI-GC, including measurement and assessment, digital transformation, and collaborative ecosystems. By embracing these research pathways, scholars can contribute to a deeper theoretical and practical understanding of BMI to address grand challenges.

**Research Question**
How can business model innovation (BMI) be harnessed to address grand challenges, and how do grand challenges in turn give rise to and enable BMI? The editorial frames this through two pathways linking strategic entrepreneurship, business model design, and the urgency of grand challenges.

**Mechanism Process**
- Core argument: BMI — fundamentally rethinking how a focal organization creates, delivers, and captures value across boundary-spanning activity systems — is a collaborative lever for resolving grand challenges that no single actor can solve alone.
- Key constructs: an "ABC" classification of grand challenges (A = artificial intelligence; B = business and global unrest; C = climate change) and a "BMI-GC" concept linking BMI to grand challenges.
- Analytical framework: a 3×3×3 framework crossing the three grand challenges (ABC) with three business-model aspects (antecedents, outcomes, processes) and three BMI-GC intersections (grand challenge as enabler of BMI opportunity; BMI to mitigate threats; BMI as enabler of societal value).
- Boundary-spanning extension: pushes the business-model construct to recognize humanity and nature as stakeholders, embed models in ecological/political/societal ecosystems, and emphasize total, multi-stakeholder value creation.

The editorial argues that BMI's boundary-spanning, system-level, activity-focused, and value-creation logic makes it especially suited to grand challenges because it can be pursued by virtually anyone and lets firms test, iterate, and scale solutions in a lean, experimental manner. Through the ABC classification and BMI-GC concept, the authors connect strategic entrepreneurship to societal impact, showing how BMI can simultaneously enhance competitive advantage and advance sustainability, resilience, and total value creation. The three Special Section papers (Esau et al., Talebian et al., Wang et al.) instantiate this by examining cognitive framing, robust action under environmental dynamism, and digital-technology-enabled business model design, respectively.

**Theoretical Contribution**
The editorial introduces the "ABC" classification and the "BMI-GC" concept as a novel conceptual toolkit, and proposes a 3×3×3 framework organizing research on BMI for grand challenges. It extends the four classic themes of business model research (boundary-spanning, system-level, activity focus, value creation) by incorporating humanity and nature as stakeholders and advancing the notion of total value creation beyond a game-theoretic frame.

**Practical Implication**
The authors outline practical strategies for managers and entrepreneurs: integrating ethical AI, embedding flexibility in business models to withstand volatility, and adopting sustainable practices that align with regulatory and market demands, illustrated through cases such as MUD Jeans (circular leasing), Kuradashi (food-waste platform), and Inditech (AI-driven pediatric education at scale).

**Limitations**
As an editorial framing a Special Section, the article is conceptual and agenda-setting rather than empirical, and the authors note that much recent conversation on these topics has occurred outside leading mainstream entrepreneurship and strategy journals, leaving the evidence base thin and several proposed cells of the framework unexamined.

**Future Research**
The editorial proposes a research agenda spanning measurement and assessment of BMI's impact on grand challenges, the role of digital transformation (e.g., LLMs, blockchain, IoT, data analytics), and collaborative ecosystems and multi-stakeholder partnerships. It identifies over 27 exemplary research questions across the ABC classification to guide future strategic-entrepreneurship inquiry.
