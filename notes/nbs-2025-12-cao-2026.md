# Biodiversity Entrepreneurship

**Abstract**
We study an emerging class of startup organizations focused on biodiversity conservation and the challenges they face in financing these ventures. By fine-tuning a large language model, we identify 630 biodiversity-linked startups in PitchBook and compare their financing dynamics with those of other ventures.

**Research Question**
The study focuses on three questions: (1) how to best classify, or create a taxonomy of, biodiversity startups and their investors; (2) what types of VC investors match with which kind of biodiversity startups; and (3) what strategies can improve the funding outcomes among VC investors for biodiversity startups.

**Mechanism Process**
- IV(s): Biodiversity (binary indicator that a startup is biodiversity-related vs. matched generic); biodiversity sector (marine/forest/agricultural/animal/multifunctional); direct vs. indirect conservation; Twitter account presence (extensive margin) and Ln(#tweets) (intensive margin); post-2017 Twitter character-limit expansion (DID shock)
- DV(s): Likelihood a deal involves at least one impact investor; likelihood a deal involves at least one public/blended investor; log deal size; total deal size; total number of financing deals
- Mediators: None formally modeled; information-asymmetry reduction via social media is the proposed channel
- Moderators: Impact-deal and blended-deal indicators (and their interactions with Biodiversity) on deal size; Biodiversity × Twitter / Biodiversity × Ln(#tweets) interactions

Biodiversity startups raise systematically smaller deals (roughly half the size of matched generic startups) yet attract a broader, hybrid coalition of capital. Relative to matched generics they have about 2.20 times the odds of securing impact-investor funding and 1.26 times the odds of attracting public investors. "Values" investors (mission-aligned impact funds and public institutions) supply incremental rather than substitutive capital, but funding gaps persist. Twitter presence is associated with larger total fundraising (~36 percent) and more deals; the proposed process is that social media reduces information asymmetry and connects ventures to "minds-unalike" value (for-profit) investors who would not otherwise engage.

**Theoretical Contribution**
The paper introduces "biodiversity startups" as a distinct, understudied object in entrepreneurial and sustainable finance, providing the first taxonomy of such ventures and of their investors. It links the sustainable-finance and entrepreneurial-finance literatures, extends Starks's (2023) values-versus-value investor distinction into private biodiversity markets, and demonstrates how alternative data and a fine-tuned LLM can identify and study an emerging phenomenon that lacks structured data.

**Practical Implication**
For policy and practice, the findings emphasize mobilizing private capital toward biodiversity preservation through hybrid and blended financing models that pair impact investors and public-sector backers with traditional VC, and through strategic communication: biodiversity ventures can use social media to raise visibility, signal credibility, and broaden their investor base beyond mission-aligned funders.

**Limitations**
The data are limited to proprietary private-market databases (PitchBook/Crunchbase) with possible reporting lags (recent founding years and deals may be underreported), and detailed project-level capital-needs data are unavailable, so observed funding differences are interpreted as a potential financing gap rather than a clean causal effect. The decision to open a Twitter account is endogenous and may reflect unobserved startup characteristics, so the authors caution against causal interpretation despite the DID design.

**Future Research**
The authors point to deeper examination of investor coalitions, the long-term performance and survival of biodiversity startups, and the broader societal impact of their nature-positive innovations. A more systematic study of biodiversity startup founders and their interdisciplinary backgrounds is also flagged as worthwhile.
