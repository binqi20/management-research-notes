# The Aesthetics of Impact: Making Impact Beautiful, Not Just Useful

**Abstract**
This commentary introduces an aesthetic reframing of social impact, offering a conceptual and methodological shift beyond prevailing metric- and output-oriented approaches. While recent scholarship has begun to view impact as a process, such work still relies on representational logics that privilege what can be demonstrated. I advance a distinct contribution by conceptualizing impact as lived, affective, and experiential—emerging through resonance, atmosphere, rhythm, and relational renewal, often before it becomes measurable. Drawing on Deweyan aesthetics, I propose a feasible framework of aesthetic inquiry that includes structured attunement, participatory aesthetic evaluation, and compositional case-making. This aesthetic turn expands the ontology, epistemology, and methodology of impact scholarship, enabling business and society research to recognize forms of significance that standard frameworks overlook.

**Research Question**
How can social-impact scholarship move beyond metric- and output-oriented framings to recognize forms of significance that are lived, affective, and experiential — and what conceptual and methodological apparatus would such an aesthetic reframing of impact require?

**Mechanism Process**
- Core argument: The dominant ontological assumption — that impact is an output awaiting demonstration — fundamentally restricts what impact is allowed to be; an aesthetic lens widens the evidentiary frame to include how individuals and communities experience change.
- Key constructs: impact as lived/affective/experiential; resonance, atmosphere, rhythm, and felt significance; the experiential registration of change.
- Framework (three practices): (1) structured aesthetic attunement — disciplined documenting of how people perceive and feel change (sensory fieldnotes, reflexive logs); (2) participatory aesthetic evaluation — those affected become co-interpretive partners, including anti-narrative attention to silences; (3) compositional case-making — assembling sensory traces and emotional turning points into an analytically interpretable account, with renewal as an aesthetic-temporal modality.
- Boundary conditions: aesthetic judgment is not arbitrary (business already relies on it in brand, architecture, ritual); metrics are not abandoned but paired with aesthetic evidence.

Drawing on Dewey's (1934) notion of art as experience, the commentary argues that organizational impact resembles artistic encounter — unfolding through form, rhythm, sensory engagement, and emotional meaning, and often felt before it is proven. The reframing shifts the field ontologically (impact as lived experience), epistemically (from "impact must be proven" to "impact is known through resonance"), and methodologically (toward sensory ethnography, atmospheric and anti-narrative inquiry, and participatory meaning-making). The researcher becomes a composer who curates and arranges diverse evidentiary registers rather than a detached verifier of predefined outcomes.

**Theoretical Contribution**
The paper advances three contributions to Business and Society scholarship: ontologically, it redirects attention from impact as a measurable outcome to impact as lived experience; epistemically, it shifts inquiry from representational logic to experiential logic; and methodologically, it expands the repertoire of impact inquiry by incorporating sensory ethnography, atmospheric analysis, (anti)narrative inquiry, and participatory meaning-making. In doing so it positions aesthetics as a distinct register for theorizing and evidencing impact beyond demonstration and measurement.

**Practical Implication**
For evaluators and organizations, the commentary offers feasible alternatives to metric-only impact assessment — structured sensory fieldnotes and observation templates, participatory and place-based testimony, and compositional case-making — that render experiential significance analytically visible without reducing it to metric proxies. It reframes the researcher/evaluator role as an active mediator who curates evidence and attends to whose voices and silences are centered.

**Future Research**
By placing experiential significance at the center of analysis, the commentary invites scholars to study atmospheres as well as outcomes, resonance as well as scale, and experience as well as evidence, arguing that increasingly complex social and environmental challenges require richer conceptual and methodological imaginations. It positions structured attunement, participatory aesthetic evaluation, and compositional case-making as an agenda for re-aestheticizing impact research.
