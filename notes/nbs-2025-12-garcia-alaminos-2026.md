# Recent global value chain reconfiguration: drivers and consequences on EU carbon footprint

**Abstract**
Our results show a change in global emissions evolution from 2008 onwards, with emerging trends of global value chains reconfiguration showing a more vital role of environmental concerns. We also find that reshoring and reoffshoring are less emission-intensive than offshoring. In the EU's context, its footprint has been reduced since the 2008 crisis, mainly due to lower emissions intensities and technical changes towards cleaner ways of production. In contrast, the geographical shift of suppliers contributes to the growth of emissions in the whole period.

**Research Question**
To what extent the recent evolution of trade flows and the GVC supplying the EU are geared towards decreasing carbon emissions, as well as the role played by reshoring strategies so far.

**Mechanism Process**
- IV(s): Geographical shifts of suppliers (offshoring, reshoring, reoffshoring relocation patterns); changes in intermediate inputs vs. final demand; technical change and emission-intensity variations.
- DV(s): Carbon footprint (consumption-based CO2 emissions) of the EU and global regions, decomposed into domestic vs. imported emissions.
- Mediators: Eight decomposition elements classified by emissions affected (direct/indirect), driver (intermediates/final demand/emission intensities), and nature of change (technical/geographical/final-demand-mix).
- Moderators: Country development level; sector type (resource-intensive, knowledge-intensive, medium/medium-low R&D manufacturing, services); period (1995-2008 vs. 2008-2018).

The paper computes carbon multipliers from an environmentally extended MRIO model and applies an additive structural decomposition (averaging two polar decompositions) to attribute changes in the EU's carbon footprint to specific drivers. By comparing a benchmark period (1995-2008) against a reporting period (2008-2018), it isolates relocation patterns and shows that reshoring and reoffshoring embody less carbon than offshoring, while geographical supplier shifts toward dirtier economies raise emissions in both periods. EU footprint reductions after 2008 stem mainly from lower emission intensities and cleaner intermediate-input technical change.

**Theoretical Contribution**
The paper extends the GVC-reconfiguration literature, which is dominated by sustainability-as-driver framings, by treating sustainability as an outcome of relocation and quantifying the carbon content of distinct relocation patterns. It widens the temporal coverage with more recent data, complements prior GVC-reconfiguration analyses with the environmental dimension, and isolates the effect of technical change on GVC restructuring within a complete additive decomposition.

**Practical Implication**
The growing relevance of imported emissions supports the adequacy of the EU's planned Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism. Because offshoring shifts toward dirtier economies persist, trade policy and alliances (e.g., Free Trade Agreements, reshoring/nearshoring/friendshoring) should embed sustainability and emissions objectives, accompanied by low-carbon technology transfers and binding responsible-sourcing standards to exploit unrealized synergies between resilience and emission-reduction goals.

**Limitations**
The analysis relies on the 2021 OECD ICIO release ending in 2018, so it cannot capture the carbon consequences of the COVID-19 pandemic or the Ukraine-Russia war. The reshoring definition is strict (requiring prior offshoring in the benchmark period), excluding cases where domestic supply rises without prior offshoring. Monetary deflation assumes similar price evolution across industries within a country.

**Future Research**
Continued monitoring of GVC trends and their carbon content is needed, including the years after 2018 not in the database, to assess the impact of the pandemic and the Ukraine-Russia war. Further work should examine how environmental issues can play a more prominent role in firms' supply and location decisions and GVC reconfiguration.
